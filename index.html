Methodology

[Image Placeholders (Edit)]
1

Femtosecond Lasering

The laser was polarized to control the growth direction of LIPSS and operated under the wavelength of 1040 nm, and a pulse of 332 fs. The optimal laser power was set to 0.05 W. Process is in Oxygen environment to reduce graphite formation.

[Image Placeholders (Edit)]
2

Acid Bath Chemical Cleaning

The cleaning solution was prepared by sequentially adding 7mL of 68% nitric acid (HNO3), 98% sulfuric acid (H2SO4), and 70% perchloric acid (HClO4). The flask is heated up to 80°C, 190°C, and 210°C to remove the graphite from the diamond.

[Image Placeholders (Edit)]
3

Water Droplet Wettability Test

10 µL water droplet is dropped on to the diamond. The contact angle between the droplet and the diamond surface is then analyzed through Image J. The smaller the contact angle, the better the surface hydrophilicity.

[Image Placeholders (Edit)]
4

Material Characterization (SEM, Raman, AFM, FIB)

LowVac environment to reduce charging and drift during SEM imaging. Raman spectroscopy to accurately observe the pure diamond and graphite composition. AFM and FIB to map and measure the overall LIPSS height and formation.

[Image Placeholders (Edit)]
5

Titanium Sputtering and Annealing

Titanium is sputtered onto the diamond substrate with a target thickness of ~800 nm. Upon completion, the sputtered diamond is then annealed at 800°C in a vacuum environment to prevent oxidation during the formation of Titanium Carbide.
